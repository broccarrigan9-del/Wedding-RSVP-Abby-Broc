My approach to marathon training and why running matters to me.

Why I Run

I started running in 7th grade, and it quickly became the sport I was naturally good at. Over time I got faster, more disciplined, and more confident. Running has always been something I could rely on. As I’ve trained for longer races like the marathon, it has become less about speed and more about consistency, patience, and mental toughness.

Marathon training has taught me how to manage my time, push through difficult workouts, and trust the long process. Every week builds on the last, and the progress only comes when I stay committed even on the days I don’t feel motivated.

Training Breakdown

  1. Workouts
    • Long runs that slowly build endurance over time
    • Easy run days focused on time on feet rather than pace
    • Occasional faster miles mixed into long runs to simulate race effort
  2. Easy Runs
    • Relaxed pace where I can talk and stay comfortable
    • Focus on getting miles on my legs without overworking my body
  3. Motivation
    • The winner t‑shirt at the Ogden Marathon… obviously
    • Personal improvement and sticking to the plan
